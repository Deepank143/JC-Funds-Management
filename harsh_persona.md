# User Persona: Harsh Jani (Owner & Supreme Admin)

## Profile Overview
* **Name**: Harsh Jani
* **Role**: Founder & Managing Director, Apex Buildcon
* **Industry**: Construction & Real Estate Development
* **Location**: Gujarat, India
* **Company Size**: Mid-sized
* **System Role**: **Owner / Admin (Hybrid Model)**

## Background & Context
Harsh is a hands-on construction firm owner. While he is deeply involved in the business, he cannot manually log every single cement bill or daily wage. He has accountants, site supervisors, or back-office staff who handle the bulk of the manual data entry. 

However, **Harsh is the ultimate authority on funds.** He needs a system that functions as a "hybrid": staff can feed the raw data (expenses, bills, milestone progress), but only Harsh (via an Admin Mode) has the authority to manage the actual funds, approve payouts, and see the big picture.

## Core Pain Points 😩
1. **Data Entry vs. Data Control**: Relying on staff to maintain Excel sheets means he loses control. He worries about data being manipulated or deleted without his knowledge.
2. **The "Who is paying what?" Problem**: Staff might collect a bill, but Harsh needs to authorize the payment. Disconnecting the bill entry from the payment authorization causes cash flow confusion.
3. **Privacy of Margins**: Harsh wants his staff to log expenses, but he *does not* want his site supervisors knowing the exact profit margin of a ₹5 Crore project.
4. **Shoulder Surfing**: When Harsh is on-site showing the app to a client or a vendor, he doesn't want them seeing his global Net Position or overall bank balances.

## Primary Goals & Motivations 🎯
* **Delegation with Absolute Control**: He wants staff to do the heavy lifting of data entry, but he wants the final say (approval) on where the money actually goes.
* **Granular Authority**: He needs an "Admin Mode" that separates him from standard users, granting him exclusive rights to edit/delete records, view global P&L, and manage sensitive client contracts.
* **Privacy on Demand**: The ability to quickly toggle Admin Mode on/off so he can safely use the app in public without exposing his company's entire financial health.

---

## How the Hybrid Model Benefits Harsh 🚀

### 1. The "Admin Mode" Toggle (Security & Privacy)
* **Benefit**: Harsh has a specific "Admin Toggle" in the app. When turned off, sensitive data (like total company profit, net position, and total bank balance) is hidden.
* **Value**: He can comfortably open the app in front of a client to show them their specific project progress, without accidentally revealing how much money the entire company is making. 

### 2. Maker-Checker Workflow (Fund Authority)
* **Benefit**: A site supervisor can log a ₹50,000 cement bill and upload the photo. However, only Harsh (in Admin Mode) can hit the "Mark as Paid" button and select which bank account the funds came from.
* **Value**: Harsh retains total control over the actual cash outflow, preventing unauthorized payments while offloading the data entry work.

### 3. Immutable Ledgers for Staff
* **Benefit**: While staff can create entries, they cannot delete or alter an expense once it is locked or approved. Only the Admin can perform overrides or deletions.
* **Value**: Total peace of mind. Harsh knows the ledgers are secure and that his staff cannot alter financial history to cover up mistakes.

### 4. Global vs. Local Dashboards
* **Benefit**: Staff only see data relevant to their assigned projects. Harsh, in Admin Mode, sees the macro-level Dashboard (Total Receivables, Payables, Net Position across all projects).
* **Value**: Protects his business intelligence while ensuring everyone has exactly the information they need to do their jobs.

## Summary for the PRD
The PRD must outline a **Hybrid, Role-Based Architecture with an Admin Override**. The system is a collaborative tool for data gathering (staff), but a strict **Command Center for fund management (Harsh)**. The implementation of an **"Admin Mode Toggle"** is critical—it acts as his digital signature for fund movement and his privacy shield when viewing the app in the field.
